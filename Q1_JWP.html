The Night Guy
by Rod Bustamante CFP®, ChFC®, CAP®, CPWA®
 
I once read a riddle that goes like this - Five frogs are sitting on a log, and four decide to jump off. How many are left on the log? I’ve posed this riddle to a number of people and most (me included) quickly answered 1! because 5 minus 4 equals 1, right?

Wrong, the correct answer is 5, because deciding is not doing.

While this may feel like a technicality in a riddle like this, it does highlight something that I believe we all intuitively know deciding is in fact different than doing. The reason I believe that we intuitively know this, is because most of us regularly decide to do things: I am going to spend more time with family, I am going to travel, get healthy, take a class, volunteer, sell my business, retire etc. and while we decide to do these things, more often than not we decide, not do.

Jerry Seinfeld had a bit on his show where he referenced himself as “Night Guy”. The gist of it was that he likes to stay up late, and that getting up after only getting 5 hours of sleep is Morning Guy’s problem because he is Night Guy, and he stays up as late as he wants.

This bit really stuck with me is because of what it emphasizes - the current version of ourselves has little to no regard for the future version of ourselves, even though it is technically the same person! For some reason we assume the future version of ourselves will miraculously act on something that the current version has continuously put off.

My challenge to those reading this article is simple don’t be Night Guy (or Gal!). Don’t let yourself wake up one day with regret for what you should have done, but like Morning Guy have no power to go back in time.

Don’t leave the future version of you to act on the things you’ve decided to do.
